A right triangle has two sides called legs which meet at a right angle and a third side called the hypotenuse. If angle A is being referenced, the side opposite this angle is usually labeled as a lower case letter a and the side or leg adjacent to the referenced angle is side b. Side a is opposite angle A, side b is opposite angle B and the hypotenuse, the side opposite the right angle C, is c. The angles of a right triangle consist of a right angle (90째) and two acute angles (an acute angle is an angle with measure less than 90째). The Pythagorean Theorem is a useful relationship that holds for all right triangles. Using our labelling system, it can be written as a2 + b2 = c2.
