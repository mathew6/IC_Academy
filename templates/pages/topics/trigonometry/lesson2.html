Properties of Sine Graph

If the equation y = sin (2x + p), is rewritten in the form y = sin (2(x + p/2)) then the horizontal shift is p/2. In general, the horizontal shift is -C/B . Notice in the figure above that the starting point of the blue sine function has been shifted to (p/2, 0). To summarize, any periodic function of the form y = A sin (Bx + C) + h has the following features. |A| = the amplitude 2p/|B| = the period h = vertical shift -C/B = the phase shift (horizontal shift)
