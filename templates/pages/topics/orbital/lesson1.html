Orbital Geometry

Basics of an Ellipse

"The orbit of every planet is an ellipse with the sun at a focus." Johannes Kepler exclaimed after observing Mars for several years and tracking its motion around the sun. Similar to planets traveling around the sun, satalleties also orbit the earth in elliptical paths. Lets first go over the make up of an ellipse:

Name Definition Key
Origin Center point of ellipses.
Foci Two fixed points on the interior of an ellipse used in the formal definition of the curve.
Semi-Major Axis The radius of an orbit at the orbit's two most distant points
Semi-Minor Axis The radius of an orbit at the orbit's two nearest points
Satellite The object orbiting Earth in an elliptical path.

As demonstrated above, the satellite (green moving circle) is traveling around the Earth which is a focus. The other focus (also referred to as the vacant focus) is another point in space symmetrical to the Earth just on the opposite side of the ellipse. We don't have to worry about the vacant focus but it is important to understand that an ellipse is made up of two foci.
